## **1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY** 

DevDash '26 is UCL's first-ever university-wide hackathon, organized by the UCL ICT Club. This document sets out the website structure and content for the event, built around a mission-command / HUD visual language — confident, urgent, and dramatic — without relying on any fictional creature or invented world. Every visitor is addressed as a future builder being briefed on a mission, not a passive event attendee. 

## **Design Goals** 

- Keep the mission-briefing tone and HUD visual system, so the site still feels distinct from a generic event page. 

- Present real event logistics (dates, times, team, prizes, FAQ) clearly and accurately — the aesthetic should never obscure practical information. 

- Drive every visitor toward a single, clear action: Register. 

- Flag every open item (eligibility rules, team size, prize breakdown, missing committee contacts) so nothing ships with placeholder text. 

## **2. THEME & CREATIVE DIRECTION** 

## **2.1 Concept** 

DevDash '26 is framed as a mission briefing rather than a conventional event page. The site speaks to the visitor as an operative being recruited for a mission: to build. There is no monster, war, or fictional year attached to this version — the drama comes from tone, pacing, and visual system, not invented lore.

## **2.3 Tone of Voice** 

- Short, confident, present-tense statements (“The mission needs builders.”). 

- Visitors are addressed directly, as builders/operatives — never as a generic audience. 

- All logistics (dates, times, eligibility, prizes) stated in plain, unambiguous language — no fictional wrapper on factual content. 

## **3. SITE ARCHITECTURE & USER FLOW** 

The site remains a single continuous scroll, structured as nine sections that move a first-time visitor from “what is this?” to “I've registered” without needing to leave the page. 

## **3.1 Full Page Flow** 

1. Loading Screen — system boot sequence 

2. Hero — headline and registration CTA 

3. Mission Brief — about DevDash '26 

4. Event Timeline — workshops and hackathon day 

5. The Arsenal — prizes 

6. Meet the Team — organizing committee 

7. War Council — FAQ 

8. Join the Program — final CTA 

9. Footer 

## **3.2 Flow Rationale** 

- Loading Screen and Hero establish tone and the core offer (10-hour university hackathon) within seconds, with Register available immediately. 

- Mission Brief builds credibility and explains who the event is for — explicitly welcoming beginners as well as experienced builders. 

- Meet the Team humanizes the event and gives participants a point of contact before Event Timeline covers the practical run-up (workshops) and the hackathon day itself. 

- The Arsenal and War Council answer “what do I get” and “what do I need to know” directly before the final ask. 

- Join the Program is the single, repeated conversion point — the same action as the Hero CTA, reinforced at the close of the page.

## **5. VISUAL DESIGN LANGUAGE** 

## **5.1 Colour Palette** 

|**Role**|**Colour**|**Usage**|
|---|---|---|
|Background|Near-black<br>(#0A0A0A)|Base canvas for all sections|
|Signal / Accent|Signal Green<br>(#1F7A3D –<br>#39FF6A)|HUD borders, glows, active states, progress<br>indicators|
|Secondary Accent|Amber (#E8A33D)|Used sparingly for callouts, hover states, or<br>placeholders|
|Text|Off-white<br>(#EAEAEA) / Grey<br>(#9A9A9A)|Primary and secondary/meta copy respectively|



## **5.2 Typography** 

- Display / Headlines: a bold, technical sans-serif (e.g. Chakra Petch, Rajdhani, or Orbitron) for HUDfacing headings. 

- Body copy: a clean, highly legible sans-serif (e.g. Inter or IBM Plex Sans) for all committee, timeline, and FAQ content. 

- System / terminal text: a monospaced face (e.g. JetBrains Mono or Space Mono) reserved for the loading sequence and small labels.

## **5.3 UI Motifs** 

- Thin neon-green borders and hexagonal or bracketed frames in place of standard rounded cards. 

- Scan-line and glow effects on load/hover states, used consistently but sparingly so real content stays easy to read. 

- Optional custom cursor (green targeting reticle) on desktop. 

## **5.4 Accessibility Considerations** 

- Maintain WCAG AA contrast between green/amber accents and the near-black background for all body text, especially in the committee and FAQ tables. 

- Provide a reduced-motion mode disabling scan-lines and pulsing effects for motion-sensitive users. 

- Keep all factual content (dates, times, contact details) in plain text — never encode it only inside a decorative graphic.

## **7. TECHNICAL RECOMMENDATIONS** 

## **7.1 Suggested Stack** 

- Framework: Next.js or Vite + React for fast iteration and easy deployment. 

- Animation: Framer Motion for scroll-triggered reveals and hover/accordion states. 

- Styling: Tailwind CSS with a small design-token layer for the HUD colour palette and typography. 

- Hosting: Vercel or Netlify for zero-friction deployment and preview links during committee review.
